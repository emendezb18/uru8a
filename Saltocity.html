Salto City

The Queen of Citrus

If you love citrus fruits and wine Salto city is your destination. This city is highly demanded for its food, and is the highest producer of juices and nectars of Uruguay. Is also the city with the most population in the country. It is located in the department of Salto on the eastern edge of the Uruguay River. This city is called Salto because the Uruguay River formed a great waterfall through the rocks at its bottom. Then a dam was built there that is very interesting to visit. If you are worried about the weather, Salto has a humid subtropical climate. Summers are hot, with maximum temperatures exceeding 40°C, while winters are cool, with temperatures sometimes falling below 0°C.
